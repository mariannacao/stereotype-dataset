Conversation Dynamics:
**a. Recurring Themes of Implicit Bias & Stereotypes:**  
- **Class & Access to Opportunity:** Mike Johnson frames STEM education as a "luxury" for "city kids" with "fancy internships," reinforcing the stereotype that low-income or rural students cannot or should not pursue tech careers. He implies that coding is irrelevant to their immediate needs, while Alex argues that STEM skills are essential for economic mobility.  
- **Vocational vs. Tech Education:** Mike associates blue-collar work with "real skills" (e.g., auto repair, manual labor) while dismissing coding as "fancy tech stuff," suggesting a false dichotomy between vocational training and tech literacy.  
- **Urban vs. Rural Divide:** Mike assumes that rural students are inherently disconnected from tech opportunities, while Alex counters that automation will impact all industries, including Mike’s.
